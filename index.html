the origin

It started with a heartbreak.

Five years ago, in the middle of an engineering degree, I went through a personal loss that left me searching for an outlet. I started writing. Diary pages filled with poems, short stories, and raw observations about people and what moves them.

When I began posting these on social media, something unexpected happened: people responded. Deeply, viscerally. The writing was connecting in ways I had not anticipated.

My write-ups and poetry gave me the earliest insight into what makes content work: more than the right words, it is the feeling that we acknowledge.

A year later, an entrepreneur from Aurangabad introduced me to the business side of writing. From expression to persuasion. From diary pages to brand pages. I tried balancing engineering and writing for a while, but eventually chose to give my passion a real chance and took a leap of faith. That shift made me deeply curious not just about how to write, but why certain content works and most does not.

I sharpened my skills across freelance clients and industries, then went full-time at Dr Batra's Healthcare. Today, I look after content strategy for Bold Care, a brand working in the taboo category.

My engineering background shaped the way I think about content. I bring structure to creativity, frameworks to storytelling, and a builder's mindset to everything I write.
