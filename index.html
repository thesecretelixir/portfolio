The Origin

It Started With a Heartbreak

Five years ago, in the middle of an engineering degree, I went through a personal loss that left me searching for an outlet. I started writing. Diary pages filled with poems, short stories, and raw observations about people and what moves them.

When I began posting these on social media, something unexpected happened: people responded. Not politely, but viscerally. The writing was connecting in ways I had not anticipated.

That was my first real lesson in content strategy: the content that connects is not the content that is technically perfect. It is the content that captures a real human tension. Every campaign I have worked on since has been built on that principle.

A year later, an entrepreneur from Aurangabad introduced me to commercial writing. That is when the shift happened. From writing as expression to writing as a business tool. I became obsessed with understanding not just how to write, but why certain content works, what makes people stop scrolling, click, trust, and buy.

I built my skills through freelance work with 20+ clients, then took on a full-time content role at Dr Batra's Healthcare where I spearheaded content strategy for a legacy brand reaching millions. Today, I lead content strategy for Bold Care and Bloom by Bold Care, two D2C brands operating in categories that most marketers would not touch.

My engineering background became my secret weapon: I approach content with systems thinking, not just creative intuition. And the heartbreak? It taught me the most important thing a strategist can know: people do not engage with information. They engage with emotion.
